Who I am

Hi, my name is Estee and I am a highly experienced and dedicated pre-k teacher with over 9 years of experience in the classroom. I have a Masters in Early Childhood Education, which has equipped me with the knowledge and skills to be a true expert in my field. ABC's of Learning

Our curriculum is specifically designed to assist your child in developing the essentials for early childhood education.
  • Bonding: Mommy and me classes provide an opportunity for mothers and their children to bond and connect with one another in a fun and interactive setting.
  • Socialization: These classes provide a space for children to interact with other children their own age and for mothers to connect with other parents.
  • Development: Mommy and me classes offer a variety of activities that can aid in the development of a child's motor skills, language skills, and cognitive abilities.
  • Support: These classes offer a supportive environment for mothers, particularly new mothers, to share their experiences, ask questions, and receive guidance and advice from other mothers.
  • Fun: Mommy and me classes are designed to be enjoyable for both the child and the parent, providing an enjoyable and memorable experience for both.

Brain Development

Through our mastery of early childhood education we are able to provide a safe and nurturing environment for your child to develop essential skills early on such as:
  • Tummy Time: Laying babies on their stomachs and encouraging them to lift their heads and push up can help strengthen their neck, shoulder, and upper body muscles.
  • Sensory Play: Introducing babies to different textures and materials, such as soft blankets, rough fabrics, and shiny objects, can help stimulate their senses and promote cognitive development.
  • Music and Movement: Playing music and dancing with babies can help develop their sense of rhythm and promote physical development.
  • Peek-a-Boo: Playing games such as peek-a-boo can help babies develop object permanence and understand the concept of cause and effect.
  • Imitation: Encouraging babies to imitate simple actions such as clapping, waving, or blowing kisses can help them develop their motor skills and improve their ability to understand and follow instructions.

General Learning

From cognitive and social development to language and physical growth, these classes offer a supportive and stimulating environment for children to explore and learn.
  • Cognitive development: Mommy and me classes can provide a variety of stimulating activities that can help babies develop their cognitive skills, such as problem-solving, memory, and spatial awareness.
  • Social and emotional development: Classes provide a space for babies to interact with other babies and adults, which can help them develop social and emotional skills, such as empathy and communication.
  • Language development: Classes often include activities such as singing songs, reading books, and talking to the babies, which can help them develop their language skills and expand their vocabulary.
  • Physical development: Classes may include activities such as movement and play that can help babies develop their gross motor skills, balance and coordination.
